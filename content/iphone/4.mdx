# iPhone 4 Bumper

Ah, the Bumper — the only Apple case born out of a public apology.

The iPhone 4 debuted a gorgeous new antenna design: stainless steel bands wrapped around the edges of the device. Users quickly discovered that holding the phone in a certain way — covering the lower-left corner where two antennas meet — caused a significant drop in signal strength. The "death grip" was born, and the whole controversy was dubbed [antennagate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPhone_4#Antennagate).

Steve Jobs held an entire press conference about it, explaining that signal attenuation was common across smartphones — just more noticeable on the iPhone 4 thanks to its antenna design. Apple's remedy was the Bumper: a case that wraps around the edges of the phone without covering the back or front, keeping your hand from touching the antenna bands directly. Every iPhone 4 owner could claim one for free, for a limited time.

The iPhone 4s arrived with a reworked antenna system, and the drama was over.
