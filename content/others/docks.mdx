# Docking Stations

Before Apple's chargers learned to float onto the back of the phone, they were little cradles that held it upright on your desk. When the iPhone moved to Lightning in 2012, the old 30-pin dock was left behind, and for a year there was no first-party stand at all.

That changed in September 2013 with two model-specific cradles — one shaped for the iPhone 5s, one for the 5c — each $29. The well of each was moulded to its exact phone, so it sat there naked, no case allowed. Around the back sat a 3.5 mm audio line-out, so a docked iPhone could pipe sound straight into powered speakers while it charged and synced.

## iPhone Lightning Dock

In May 2015 Apple replaced the shape-specific cradles with a single, universal dock in white for $39. Freed from hugging one body, it charged any Lightning iPhone — and Lightning iPods too — while keeping the 3.5 mm line-out on the back.

That September, alongside the iPhone 6s, it turned vain: anodised aluminium finishes to match the phones themselves, at $49 each. A **Black** dock followed in 2016 for the iPhone 7, and a fresh **Gold** in 2017 for the iPhone 8 and X era. For a few years you could colour-match the dock on your desk to the phone in your hand — a lovely, forgotten detail Apple eventually let lapse as the world moved to wireless.
