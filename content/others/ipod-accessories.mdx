# iPod Accessories

## This page is under construction!

Before Apple dressed iPhones in silicone and leather, it dressed iPods — in armbands, lanyards, tubes, and, famously, socks. Between 2003 and 2013, Apple shipped a small wardrobe's worth of first-party iPod accessories, most of which have long since been forgotten.

This page is a working inventory of every Apple-made iPod accessory catalogued so far. For now it's just a list, not proper catalogue entries — no photos, no regional variants yet. Prices are the original US MSRP where one could be sourced; a — means no defensible number has been found yet.

**A work in progress:** this list is almost certainly incomplete. Confirmed gaps are noted in their sections below, and there's surely more out there — if it has an Apple part number and clipped, wrapped, or docked an iPod, it belongs here.

## iPod Socks

The one that started it all? I wonder how many times these words appear throughout this wiki.

On November 4, 2004, Apple released the **M9720G/B** — a set of six iPod socks that became an instant sensation. What's particularly nice about them is that you could get the entire set for just $29, so you didn't have to worry about choosing which ones to buy — you could just use whichever sock you liked.

For a pleasing video on the subject, check out [this clip](https://www.youtube.com/embed/G8Rt4qxKMyA) by Stephen Hackett

For an excellent read on iPod Socks, be sure to check out this [piece](https://basicappleguy.com/basicappleblog/ipod-socks) by Based Apple Guy; it tells the story better than I could ever do.

In addition to the standard six-pack, there was also an exclusive red iPod sock that was never made available for purchase. This limited-edition sock did not have a part number and was gifted to customers during the opening of the [Boylston Apple Store](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2008/05/14The-Apple-Store-Boylston-Street-to-Open-Thursday-May-15/) on May 15, 2008. Here's a nice story about the red sock, again by Stephen: [The Forgotten iPod Sock](https://512pixels.net/2016/11/the-forgotten-ipod-sock/).

_On February 23, 2005, alongside the second-generation mini, Apple rolled out a whole set of neoprene armbands in bold colours at $29 each — press coverage from the time names orange, yellow, blue, and pink joining the earlier grey. Only the grey (M9838) and yellow (**M9864**) part numbers are confirmed so far; the orange, blue, and pink SKUs from that launch are still being tracked down and are omitted rather than guessed._

## iPod shuffle

The original "wear it" iPod. The shuffle plugged straight into a USB port, so Apple built a matching set of things to hang it off your body — a lanyard in the box, plus a neoprene armband and a water-resistant sport case sold separately.

| MA694 | iPod shuffle Dock (2nd generation)      | Autumn 2006  | $29  |
| MC003 | iPod shuffle USB Cable (3rd generation) | Spring 2009  | $19  |

_The Sport Case (M9758G/A) was clear plastic sealed with an orange rubber gasket and a built-in neck lanyard — water-resistant, and designed so you could work the controls without opening it. The Armband (M9760G/A) was the same light-grey neoprene as the mini's, cradling the shuffle by its USB plug._

## iPod Hi-Fi

And then there's the big one. Announced in February 2006 at $349, the **iPod Hi-Fi (M9867)** was Apple's attempt at a living-room speaker dock — killed off barely eighteen months later. It also shipped in a Japan-specific variant (M9867J). One day it'll deserve a page of its own.
