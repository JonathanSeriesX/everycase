# MagSafe Charging

MagSafe — the name resurrected in 2020 from Apple's old magnetic MacBook chargers — is a ring of magnets on the back of every iPhone since the 12, and a small family of accessories that snap onto it.

## MagSafe Charger

The puck that started it. The first model (2020, $39) delivered up to 15 W over a captive 1 m USB-C cable, with no power brick in the box. In 2024 it was reborn with a native USB-C connector and a braided cable in two lengths — 1 m ($39) and 2 m ($49) — and up to 25 W on the iPhone 16. The 2025 refresh looks identical but earns full Qi2 25 W certification, delivering the full 25 W to any Qi2.2 device, not just an iPhone.

## MagSafe Duo Charger

A folding travel charger (2020, $129) combining an iPhone pad and an Apple Watch puck. It never quite reached 15 W, topping out around 11–14 W, and was quietly discontinued in September 2023.

## MagSafe Battery Pack

Apple's snap-on power bank (2021, $99). It shipped charging at 5 W on the go, then a firmware update in 2022 raised that to 7.5 W, and it worked in both directions — the iPhone could top up the pack, or the pack the iPhone. It was retired in September 2023 when the iPhone 15 dropped Lightning for USB-C.

## iPhone Air MagSafe Battery

The battery returned in 2025, after two years away — an ultra-thin pack shaped to the iPhone Air's footprint and, for now, sold only for the Air. It adds up to about 65% more charge and stretches the Air's video playback toward 40 hours.
