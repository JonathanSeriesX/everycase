# MagSafe Charging accessories

MagSafe — the name resurrected in 2020 from Apple's old magnetic MacBook chargers — refers to a ring of magnets on the back of [almost](https://www.macrumors.com/2025/02/28/apple-provides-reason-iphone-16e-no-magsafe/) every iPhone since the iPhone 12, and a family of accessories that snap onto it.

## MagSafe Charger

v1 — 15W, boring cable

v2 — 25W on iPhone 16 and up, Qi2 15W on Android, braided cable

v3 — 25W on iPhone 16 and up, Qi 2.2 25W on Android, braided cable

## MagSafe Duo Charger

A folding travel charger combining a MagSafe charging pad and an Apple Watch puck. Charging speeds topped out at around 11–14 W, and it was quietly discontinued in September 2023.

## MagSafe Battery Pack

Apple's snap-on power bank. It shipped charging at 5 W on the go, then a firmware update in 2022 [raised that to 7.5 W](https://www.macrumors.com/2022/04/20/magsafe-battery-pack-faster-charging/), and it worked in both directions — the iPhone could top up the pack, or the pack the iPhone.

## iPhone Air MagSafe Battery

The battery returned in 2025, after two years away — an ultra-thin pack shaped to the iPhone Air's footprint and, for now, sold only for the Air. It adds up to about 65% more charge and stretches the Air's video playback toward 40 hours.
